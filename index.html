Reference Notes

Urgency Definitions

C Critical

V Corps G33 will provide initial 5W (verbal report) to UR-AF Watch Desk within 30 minutes of notification.

I Immediate

V Corps Watch Officer / G33 provide initial (5W) voice report to the USAREUR-AF Watch Officer within one (1) hour of notification.

R Routine

Units submit voice report to the G33 Watch Officer / Battle Desk within 1 hour of notification of incident.

Reporting Criteria (Notification Levels)

WU Wake-Up — 24/7 Immediate

Voice report 24/7 and immediate release of the CII. The Watch Officer will:
• Provide voice report to the G33 within one (1) hour of receiving the initial report and will continue notification until told to stop.
• Publish the CII within two (2) hours following voice report.

FL First Light — Normal Duty Day

Voice report (normal duty-day only, 0700–1700). Reports received 1701–0659: Watch Officer will provide verbal report on/about 0700 and publish the CII before 0900. Reports received after 0700: Watch Officer will provide verbal notification to G33 within 30 minutes and publish the CII within two (2) hours of notification.

R Routine

Routine — daily end-of-day email roll-up, to include weekends and holidays.

D Daily Roll-Up

Daily end-of-day email roll-up, to include weekends and holidays.

** Email Only

Incident will be reported via email from the V Corps Watch Officer / Staff Duty Officer to the USAREUR-AF G3 Watch Officers. G3 Chief of Operations will provide a daily roll-up to the USAREUR-AF CG.

General Reporting Notes

• Staff Duty Officers/XOs/DCOs will ensure the local Garrison Provost Marshal/MP Office is notified of all items that involve criminal activity and/or meet SIR criteria. The Provost Marshall Chain is responsible for generating and submitting the SIR, not the reporting unit.

• Staff Duty Officers/XOs/DCOs will ensure the unit level Safety Officer is notified of any accidents or injuries that require reporting per DP PAM 385-40. The unit level Safety Officer will then notify the USAREUR Safety Office and ensure the appropriate reports are submitted. Class A–B Ground Accidents and A–C Aviation Accidents are reportable CCIR as defined by this matrix.

• Casualties are reported IAW AR 600-8-1, AER 600-8-1, and AE Pamphlet 600-8-1. MSC/BCT Staff Duty Officers/XOs/DCOs will initiate appropriate casualty reporting through their S1 shop and will ensure the USAREUR G1 and Casualty Affairs Office are notified as required.
